FEEDING GUIDELINES
Dogs should be fed 2 times per day, morning and evening. Puppies under 6 months need 3 meals per day.
Cats should be fed 2-3 times per day. Avoid leaving wet food out for more than 30 minutes.
Always provide fresh water. Change water bowl daily.
Avoid feeding pets: chocolate, grapes, onions, garlic, xylitol, avocado.

MEDICATION GUIDELINES
Always give medication at the same time each day for consistency.
Never skip a dose without consulting a vet.
Some medications must be given with food to avoid stomach upset.
Store medications in a cool dry place away from pets.

EXERCISE GUIDELINES
Dogs need at least 30 minutes of exercise per day. High energy breeds need 60-90 minutes.
Cats need 10-15 minutes of active play twice daily.
Avoid intense exercise right after feeding — wait at least 1 hour.
Senior pets need gentler, shorter exercise sessions.

GROOMING GUIDELINES
Brush dogs 2-3 times per week minimum. Long haired breeds need daily brushing.
Cats generally self-groom but benefit from weekly brushing.
Trim nails every 3-4 weeks for dogs, every 4-6 weeks for cats.
Bathe dogs monthly unless they get dirty. Cats rarely need baths.

HEALTH AND VET VISITS
Annual vet checkups are recommended for all pets.
Senior pets over 7 years should visit the vet every 6 months.
Keep vaccinations up to date — rabies, distemper, and flea prevention are essential.
Watch for warning signs: lethargy, loss of appetite, excessive thirst, vomiting.

SCHEDULING BEST PRACTICES
Keep feeding times consistent — pets thrive on routine.
Space walks and exercise at least 4 hours apart when possible.
Never schedule two high priority tasks at the same time for the same pet.
Morning routines should include feeding before exercise.
